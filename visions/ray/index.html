Visions / Ray, Satyajit

Ten films across thirty-six years — a cross-film reading of Satyajit Ray’s cinematic life through ten allegorical events. The films are ordered chronologically as stages of a single river, each carrying all ten events simultaneously, one dominant at each stage. The events form the river’s current: the world first beheld, everything wagered on it, a call received, the watching that cannot intervene, the face beneath the performance, the refusal that defines the self, the ending met with dignity, power written into the mind, the irrevocable crossing, the return that finds nothing restored.
